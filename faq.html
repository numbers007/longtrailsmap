Longtrailsmap.net FAQ

Q: Can I have a copy of the GPS file for a particular trail?

A: For routes where the license permits redistribution, download links are on the 'To Do' list. However, some GPS data has been made available to Longtrailsmap.net on the condition that it be made available only in map form, and not available for direct download. Note that most of the GPS info on Longtrailsmap.net is crowdsourced (and so precision is unconfirmed), can be several years out of date, and, sometimes, is slightly adjusted for presentation purposes. For these and other reasons, it is not suitable for use in navigation, and should be used only for inspirational and entertainment purposes.

Q: How do you decide what trails to include on the map?

A: The following criteria are general guidelines, though there are some exceptions.
  • The trail...
    • ...is at least 50 miles long.
    • ...involves minimal road walks.
    • ...is mostly completed.
    • ...is mostly in wild areas with natural earth for trail surface.
    • ...is primarily a hiking trail and is designed for foot traffic.
    • ...can be completed without substantial mountaineering skills or pack rafting.
    • ...is not under regulations that require hiking with guides.

Q: Where do you get the GPS data for the map?

A: Almost all of the paths shown on the map come from public data accessible from links on the first page (or two) of a Google search.
  • Some good sources for lists of existing trails:
    • American Long Distance Hiking Association - West
    • Caltopo
    • Gaiagps.com
    • Hiking.waymarkedtrails.org
    • Hikingproject.com
    • NRTDatabase.org
    • The individual sites of the agencies that steward the trails.
    • Traildino
    • Wikiloc
    • Wikipedia: Long Distance Trails in the United States, and other articles.
    • www.outdooractive.com (Europe)

Q: What technology do you use in developing the site?

A: The site itself is simple handwritten static web standards served from S3. The map is hosted by Mapbox.com courtesy of the awesome Mapbox Community Program, and uses Mapbox's proprietary tilesets. API access is handled through Mapbox GL JS. QGIS, JOSM, Caltopo.com, and GPSVisualizer.com are the main tools used to process the GPS data for presentation.
